TOOTH AVULSION
One of the commonest sequelae of facial trauma is tooth avulsion, exfoliation or articulation.
Salient features
ď‚· History of fall, interpersonal violence, sports injury, assault or accident.
ď‚· Children are more common.
ď‚· Central incisors and developing teeth are more frequently avulsed.
ď‚· Patient presents extreme pain with a bleeding socket, clot in the socket and a raw
wound with or without Lip & Labial mucosa injury.
Treatment
ď‚· Immediately refer to a dentist. Best result is observed if tooth is reimplanted within 5-20
minutes.
ď‚· Fixation of implanted tooth with periodontal wiring, arch bar wiring or composite resin;
fixation period 6 to 8 weeks; root canal treatment done after reimplantation only (to avoid
desiccation of periodontal ligament).
Interim storage
ď‚· Best method is to place back the tooth in the socket immediately. Other storage media are
saliva, milk (placed in ice since this minimizes the adverse effects on the periodontal
ligament) and saline.
Pharmacological treatment
ď‚· Cap. amoxycillin 250-500 mg 3 times a day for 5 days Or Tab. ciprofloxacin 250-500 mg
twice a day for 5 days.
ď‚· Tab. diclofenac Potassium 50 mg b.d. or Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg 3 times for 3-5 days.
TOOTHACHE
Direct causes are dental caries, periodontal pocket, abrasion, attrition & erosion, peridontitis,
third molar eruption (Impacted Tooth). The indirect causes of toothache are maxillary
sinusitis (recent bout of common cold) & trigeminal neuralgia.
Pharmacological treatment
ď‚· Cap. amoxycillin 250 -500 mg 3 times a day for 5 days. Or Tab. ciprofloxacin 250 -500
mg 2 times a day for 5 days.
ď‚· Tab. diclofenac potassium 50 mg b.d. or Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg 3 times for 3-5 days.
Surgical treatment
ď‚· Excavation of caries and sedative dressing with clove oil. Removal of plaque & calculus
by scaling & local medicaments. Removal of irritant like high filling and high spot on
crown or bridge. Anaesthetize the tooth and extirpate the pulp (if pulp is exposed).

DENTAL CARIES

demineralization of inorganic and destruction of organic part of the tooth. (Enamel & Dentin)
Salient features
ď‚· Usually asymptomatic in early stages.
ď‚· Patient presents with tooth sensitivity and tooth ache only on cold & sweet intakes

Treatment
ď‚· Examine for stage of caries and treat accordingly.
ď‚· Careful assessment of oral cavity for presence of any white/ brown or black spot.
Non-pharmacological treatment
ď‚· In non-cavitated lesion and low risk patient with good oral hygiene practices, no
treatment is given.
ď‚· In cavitated lesion, restoration is done.
Pharmacological treatment
ď‚· Patients with caries is likely to progress (in high risk patient) pit and fissure sealant.
ď‚· Topical 2% sodium fluoride.

DENTAL ABSCESS
Patient presents with pain and swelling. The most common types of dental abscesses are
periapical abscess and lateral periodontal abscess.
I. PERIAPICAL ABSCESS
Salient features
ď‚· Presence of caries or trauma. Severe throbbing pain, disturbed sleep
ď‚· Tooth is tender to touch & extruded from socket. Tooth may be mobile and associated
with localized or diffuse swelling.
Immediate treatment
ď‚· To give antibiotics as given below and refer to a dentist.
Pharmacological treatment
ď‚· Cap. amoxycillin 250 -500 mg 3 times a day for 5 days. Or Tab. ciprofloxacin 250-500
mg two times a day for 5 days.
ď‚· Tab. diclofenac potassium 50 mg b.d. or Tab. ibuprofen 400 mg 3 times a day for 3-5
days.

II. LATERAL PERIDONTAL ABSCESS
Salient features
ď‚· Same as in acute periapical abscess,often associated with bad taste.

ď‚· Vitality test usually positive if no associated pulpal problem.
ď‚· Radiograph shows vertical or horizontal bone loss in relation to the tooth.

Patient education
ď‚· Maintenance of oral hygiene. No hot fomentation over the skin.Control of diabetes

JUVENILE PERIODONTITIS
It is characterized by rapid destruction of periodontal tissues.
Salient features
ď‚· Common in the age group of 13-25 years.
ď‚· Mobility in incisors and molars, spacing in upper incisors, distolabial migration of

to mesial surface of second molar.
Pharmacological treatment
ď‚· Cap. tetracycline 250 mg 4 times a day for 14 days.
Surgical treatment
ď‚· Extraction of badly involved teeth. Refer the patient to periodontist for further
periodontical management at the earliest
Patient education
ď‚· Proper brushing twice daily with super soft tooth brush.

GENERAL MEASURES FOR GOOD ORAL HYGIENE
ď‚· Select the right quality of tooth brush which should be short, soft and have uniformly
trimmed bristles. Change the brush at least after every 3 months
ď‚· Brush teeth at least twice a day for 2-3 minutes particularly at night before going to sleep.
ď‚· Use right technique of teeth brushing. Never use force while brushing. Use flossing at
least once a day.
ď‚· Avoid too much sugar and aerated drinks. Avoid eating in between meals, if cannot be
avoided rinse your mouth or preferably brush your teeth.
ď‚· Do not ingest or swallow fluorinated toothpaste while brushing.
ď‚· Ensure regular dental checkup at 6 monthly interval.
